What is Kisspeptin-10?

Kisspeptin-10 (KP-10) is the biologically active C-terminal decapeptide fragment of kisspeptin-54, a neuropeptide encoded by the KISS1 gene and produced primarily in the hypothalamus. It is the most potent endogenous activator of GnRH (gonadotropin-releasing hormone) neurons, making it a master regulator of the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis. Kisspeptin-10 research has expanded significantly in reproductive biology, puberty onset, fertility, and testosterone regulation studies.
