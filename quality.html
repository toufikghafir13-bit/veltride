What Is a Certificate of Analysis (COA)?

A Certificate of Analysis is a document provided by an independent, accredited third-party testing laboratory that certifies the identity, purity, and quality of a compound. For research-grade compounds, the COA documents:

HPLC purity percentage • Batch/lot number • Compound identity • Date of analysis • Appearance and physical characteristics • Water content

Every VELTRIDE shipment includes a COA specific to the batch you received, allowing you to verify the purity of your exact sample against documented test results.

Understanding Batch Numbers

Each batch of compound is assigned a unique lot number (e.g., VT-BPC). This number links your physical product to the specific production run and its associated COA. If you receive a vial labelled Lot VT-BPC, your COA will also reference VT-BPC, confirming the purity data applies to your exact sample.

Batch numbers enable traceability from synthesis through third-party testing to delivery. Contact us with your lot number for specific COA reference information.

HPLC Purity Explained

High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) is the industry-standard analytical method used to measure compound purity. HPLC separates compound components and quantifies the target compound as a percentage of the total sample. A purity of ≥99% means at least 99% of the sample is the target compound.

VELTRIDE maintains a minimum purity standard of ≥99% HPLC across all active catalogue items. Batches below this threshold are rejected and not released.

Third-Party Testing

All VELTRIDE compounds are tested by independent, accredited third-party laboratories. This means purity verification is conducted by a laboratory with no commercial interest in the outcome, providing an objective quality assessment. Third-party testing is the gold standard for research-grade compound documentation.
